# 1. Life begins at the edge of your comfort zone #

Nice cliché to start things with, I know. But it's as true as they come. Your comfort zone represents a place of familiarity and security

Seems like not so long ago I turned up at my local SES Unit for an Information Night for prospective volunteers. The concept was *very* different to anything I'd ever done before, and I clearly remember the fluttering anxiety. In my head, the little voice of the comfort zone suggested "Just skip out on this, go home, and play Mass Effect 2" (man, that's been 10 years?!). Even before seeing what it was all about, my inclination was to slide back to my comfort zone. Pulling my head out of my ass, I pressed on.

Volunteering in the SES fundamentally changed my life, and my perceptions of myself and my capabilities.
