## Short Professional Bio ##

Stephen Sennett is a senior technical professional, specializing in Cloud Computing and Technical Architecture. He is certified and experienced across numerous technology platforms, and has been recognized as an [AWS Community Builder](https://aws.amazon.com/developer/community/community-builders/). Additionally, he serves his community passionately as a committed Rotarian, and experienced volunteer across the Emergency Management sector, having served in numerous during several major Australian disasters.

He currently works with [A Cloud Guru](https://acloudguru.com/) as a Content Remediation Architect.

## Personal Bio ##

I do things and solve stuff to make the world a better and more interesting place.

**Professional** hobbies include solving issues at a large-scale, whether it's designing and building technical solutions that can help people around the world, or solving procedural issues that can eliminate unnecessary red-tape and make teams more effective. I'm a huge fan of cloud technology and the opportunities it presents for taking an idea from a thought, to a basic prototype, to a global solution capable of serving millions of people in a matter of hours. 

**Rotary** is a major part of my life, serving as a member of the [Rotary E-Club of Melbourne](https://rotaryeclubofmelbourne.org.au/) in various roles, including as President-Nominee for 2023-24. Beyond my club, I also serve in various committees of Rotary District 9800, Action Groups, and Fellowships, since our world in Rotary is far larger than our own club. As an ex-Rotaractor, I remain deeply committed to supporting our next generation of leaders through working with clubs and their members, and currently serve as the Rotarian Advisor for the [Rotaract E-Club of Melbourne](https://www.facebook.com/emelbournerotaract/), the first Rotaract E-Club in Australia.

**Emergency Management** started with joining my local State Emergency Service unit in 2011 as a Rescue Operator, and grew to managing the unit as Deputy Unit Controller. From there, I began serving with the Australian Red Cross Emergency Services, primarily leading the Planning Section of Agency Incident Management Teams at State and National-level, but also serving as a Liaison to other agencies. Outside of active operations, I work with our partners internally and externally in the Preparedness and Recovery phases, and serve as a State Trainer with a focus on Incident Management and Leadership.
